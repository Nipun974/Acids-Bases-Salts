ACIDS BASES AND SALTS

This website helps to understand the basics of what are acids, bases and salts.



The materials can be classified on the basis of their various properties such as colour, lustre, hardness, roughness, solubility and transparency. Materials can also be classified into three groups namely- ACIDS, BASES, SALTS on the basis of their taste.

ACIDS

  • They are sour in taste.
  • They are the substances which produce H+ ions in a solution.
  • There are two types of acids based on their sources, that is Organic and Inorganic.
  • Organic Acids- Citric Acid, Lactic Acid, Ascorbic Acid.
  • Inorganic Acids- Sulphuric Acid, Hydrochloric Acid, Carbonic Acid.
  • They are also classified on the basis of their strength, that is weak and strong acids.
  • Acids are corrosive in nature and good conductors of electricity.
  • Few examples of acids are- Sulphuric Acid(Strong Acid), Citric acid in citrus fruits(Weak Acid), Lactic Acid in milk .etc

    • BASES

  • They are the substances which produce or OH- ions in a solution.
  • They are bitter in taste and give a soapy feeling when touched.
  • Few of the bases can be dissolved in water and are called alkalis. Eg-Sodium hydroxide, Potassium hydroxide etc.
  • They are corrosive in nature. Eg- Cupric Hydroxide, Ferric Hydroxide.

    • SALTS

  • Substances that are neither acidic or basic are called neutral substances or salts.
  • They are the product formed when a acid reacts with a base.
  • Eg- NaCl, NH4F,MgCO3....etc.

    • NEUTRALISATION REACTION

  • The reaction in which an acid completely reacts with a base to form a salt and water is called neutralisation.
  • It is a exothermic reaction





    • ACID + BASE → SALT + WATER

    HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O
